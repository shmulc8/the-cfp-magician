# Reversim Summit CFP Guidelines

Reversim Summit (רברסים) is one of Israel's premier developer conferences. It has a highly competitive CFP process with unique dynamics. Use these guidelines when coaching a user who is submitting to Reversim.

---

## 🗳️ The Core Dynamic: Public Voting & Committee Selection

Reversim proposals must clear two hurdles:
1.  **Public Community Voting:** The general community rates talks online. Voters scroll through hundreds of proposals, often on their phones.
2.  **Content Committee Curation:** The committee reviews the highest-voted proposals to select the final lineup, balancing tracks and formats.

### Designing for Both:
*   **For the Public Voter (The Scroll):** 
    *   The **Title** must be catchy but immediately clear. If a voter doesn't understand the topic in 2 seconds, they will scroll past or rate it low.
    *   The **Hook (first sentence)** of the abstract must grab attention immediately and relate to problems developers face daily.
*   **For the Committee (The Deep Dive):**
    *   The **Abstract** and **Notes to Committee** must demonstrate technical depth, feasibility, and proof of capability. 
    *   Include concrete metrics, architecture references, or links to working code.

---

## 🇮🇱 Israeli Developer Culture & Tone

The Reversim audience consists of highly technical, opinionated developers.
*   **Authenticity over Marketing:** Avoid corporate buzzwords, excessive marketing speak, or high-level management jargon. They want to hear about real engineering pain, mistakes, and technical trade-offs.
*   **"Show Me the Code":** Live demos, open-source repos, and hard metrics (e.g., "we cut latency by 40%") perform significantly better than theoretical concepts.

---

## 🗣️ Bilingual Framing (Hebrew & English)

Reversim accepts proposals and talks in both Hebrew and English.
*   **Language Choice:** Ask the user which language they prefer to present in. 
*   **Hebrew Submissions:**
    *   Use a professional yet conversational tone. Avoid overly formal academic Hebrew, but also avoid writing entirely in English transliteration.
    *   Keep technical terms in English (e.g., *RAG, LLM, Latent Space*) but write the surrounding structure in natural Hebrew.
*   **English Submissions:**
    *   Ensure the abstract is polished and natural. Avoid direct translations of Hebrew idioms (e.g., translate "לחיות בסרט" or "כאב ראש" to their appropriate English professional equivalents).

---

## ⚡ Format Options: Lightning Talks (5 mins)

If a user has a niche topic, a cool open-source project, or a quick, punchy story, steer them toward a **5-minute lightning talk**.
*   **Focus:** A lightning talk should cover exactly *one* idea or tool.
*   **Style:** Fast-paced, high energy, and highly visual.
